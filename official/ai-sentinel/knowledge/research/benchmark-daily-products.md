# Benchmark Daily Products

## Why this belongs in the KB

The daily brief format should not be an arbitrary editorial choice. It should be anchored in real benchmark products that show what good scan-friendly intelligence delivery looks like.

## Benchmarked products

### TLDR

- strength: radical simplicity
- useful lesson: keep structure clean and low-friction
- risk if copied blindly: too little editorial judgment

### The Rundown AI

- strength: clear "What -> So What -> Now What" pattern
- useful lesson: consequence framing must be as visible as facts
- best use in AI Sentinel: lead-story backbone

### Morning Brew

- strength: pacing and progressive reading rhythm
- useful lesson: the brief should feel intentionally paced rather than uniform blocks of text
- best use in AI Sentinel: rhythm and scan design

### Superhuman AI

- strength: extreme brevity and speed
- useful lesson: tight time budget matters
- risk if copied blindly: under-explains high-impact developments

### 量子位

- strength: strong Chinese market context and detailed local reporting
- useful lesson: the China ecosystem needs dedicated visibility
- risk if copied blindly: too much depth for a 3-5 minute scan

## Packaged editorial conclusion

AI Sentinel should combine:

- The Rundown AI structure
- Morning Brew pacing
- a China-aware layer for local ecosystem context

The result is:

- one lead item
- a compact set of secondary items
- explicit consequence framing
- a readable mobile cadence
